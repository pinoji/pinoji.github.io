Physical Chemistry Through Computer Simulations #

I am an Associate Professor of Physical Chemistry at Arkansas State University. My research involves developing high-performance computational methodologies to quantify complex molecular and biomolecular interactions.

We utilize ab initio and DFT techniques, with a focus on molecular dynamics simulations to study binding mechanics in enzyme-inhibitor complexes within highly flexible protein environments.
