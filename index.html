Physical Chemistry Through Computer Simulations #

I teach Physical Chemistry at Arkansas State University. My research focuses on developing high-performance computational methodologies to quantify complex molecular and biomolecular interactions.

Our current work involves ab initio and DFT techniques, with a central interest in molecular dynamics simulations of large biological complexes. Specifically, we are elucidating binding mechanics in enzyme-inhibitor complexes within highly flexible protein environments.
